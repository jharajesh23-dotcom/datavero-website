Refund / Revision Policy

This policy explains how revisions, cancellations, and refund requests are generally handled for DataVero projects.

Last updated: 23 June 2026DataVero Analytics & Solutions

1. Revisions

Revision terms should be agreed before the project begins. A revision usually means improving or correcting work within the original agreed scope.

2. What Counts as a Revision

  • Minor layout changes in dashboards or reports.
  • Correction of formulas, labels, visuals, or formatting.
  • Adjustment of charts, filters, KPI cards, or summary views within the agreed scope.

3. Scope Changes

New dashboards, new datasets, new data sources, major redesigns, additional automation, or a different business question may be treated as extra work and may require a new quote.

4. Cancellation

If a project is cancelled before work starts, refund options may be discussed based on payment method and administrative cost. If work has already started, charges may apply for completed time and deliverables.

5. Refunds

Refund eligibility depends on project stage, agreed scope, completed work, and reason for the request. Completed and approved work is generally not refundable.

6. Client Review Timeline

Clients are encouraged to review delivered files promptly and share clear feedback so revisions can be completed efficiently.

7. Contact

For revision or refund questions, contact: official.datavero@gmail.com

For best results, confirm revision count, payment terms, and delivery expectations in writing before starting each project.
